Why This Does Not Exist Anywhere

Every POS system built end-of-day for the finance team reviewing data the next morning. Nobody built it for the manager closing the restaurant at 11pm.

Those are two completely different users with two different jobs and two different emotional states. Toast has the finance report. Close Night Mode is what the manager needs.

Why Cards Instead of a Report

A report demands attention. It asks the owner to find the information they need inside a wall of numbers. A card gives one piece of information at a time and asks for a single response: next or approve.

The owner at 7am has 30 seconds before the day starts. Four cards is 30 seconds. A report is 15 minutes. Same data. Completely different experience.

The Four Cards — Why These

1
The Mood
Was it a good night or not. Owner knows in 3 seconds whether to be calm or alert.
2
The Number
One sales figure with last week comparison. Everything the owner needs to know about revenue.
3
The Flags
Anything unusual from the manager's exception log. Walkouts, voids, notes. One tap to review.
4
The Decision
Approve and move on or flag for follow-up. One tap. Done. Day starts.
How This Connects to Close Night Mode

The manager's exceptions from Close Night Mode become the flags in the Owner Morning Cards. The cash count becomes the reconciliation note. The two products feed each other. Close Night Mode captures the data. Owner Morning Cards surfaces it in the right form for the right person at the right time.
